Two Conjectures on EqCat∞

Open questions about equational reasoning in higher categories

Conjecture 1: Weak Adequacy — Do the axioms characterize (∞,1)-categories?
Conjecture 2: Eff Model — Does the effective topos support an internal ∞-category?

Based on "Equational Reasoning in ∞-Categories"
(Anonymous, LICS '26)

Background: The Theory EqCat∞

EqCat∞ is a compact, typed equational theory for ∞-categories. Its language has:

  • n-cells at every dimension n ≥ 0
  • src, tgt — source and target operators (dimension n+1 → n)
  • id — identity operator (dimension n → n+1)
  • ˆk — composition through a shared k-dimensional boundary

The axioms IC1–IC13 encode globularity, typing of composition, associativity, exchange, unit laws, and the interaction of identity with composition.

The 13 Axioms (IC1–IC13)

Globularity: IC1–IC2 — src and tgt compose correctly across dimensions

Identity typing: IC3–IC4 — src(id a) = a, tgt(id a) = a

Composition typing: IC5–IC8 — src and tgt of compositions

Associativity: IC9 — (z ˆk y) ˆk x = z ˆk (y ˆk x)

Exchange: IC10 — two composition operators at different levels interchange

Unit laws: IC11–IC12 — composing with an identity gives back the original cell

Id & composition: IC13 — id(y ˆk x) = id y ˆk+1 id x

These 13 axioms are the entire theory. Everything else must follow from them.

What Is Already Proved

Theorem 6.1 — Strong Adequacy

Under the strong reading, where every equation is a literal identity, the models of EqCat∞ are exactly the strict ω-categories.

Both directions are proved. The axioms are complete.

Additionally, the paper constructs a homotopy model (Section 7) and proves soundness (Section 8): (∞,1)-categories satisfy the axioms under the weak reading.

What is missing: the converse for the weak reading. Does satisfying the axioms weakly suffice to be an (∞,1)-category?

The Weak Reading

In the strong reading, an equation a = b between n-cells means:

a and b are the same n-cell. Literally identical.

In the weak reading, an equation a = b between n-cells means:

There exists an (n+1)-dimensional cell φ connecting a to b.
The equation holds up to a witness one dimension higher.

The witness φ is itself a cell, and its equations with other cells are witnessed by (n+2)-cells, and so on — witnesses all the way up.

What Is an (∞,1)-Category?

An (∞,1)-category is an ∞-category where:

  • 0-cells (objects) — no invertibility requirement
  • 1-cells (morphisms) — no invertibility requirement
  • 2-cells and above — all invertible

Intuition from topology: given a space X, paths (1-cells) have a direction, but homotopies between paths (2-cells) and all higher structure are always reversible.

Standard models include quasicategories (Joyal/Lurie), complete Segal spaces (Rezk), and Segal categories — all known to be equivalent.

The Weak Adequacy Conjecture

Conjecture

Consider the EqCat∞ axioms augmented with the requirement that all cells of dimension ≥ 2 are invertible. Under the weak reading — where an equation between n-dimensional terms is witnessed by an (n+1)-dimensional cell rather than being a strict identity — the models of this theory are exactly the (∞,1)-categories.

This requires proving two directions:

Soundness: Every (∞,1)-category satisfies the axioms. (Largely done — Sections 7 & 8 of the paper.)
Completeness: Every model satisfying the axioms is an (∞,1)-category. (Open.)

The Central Difficulty: The Coherence Tower

An (∞,1)-category requires an infinite tower of coherence data:

LevelWhat lives hereExample
0 Operations Composition, identity, src, tgt
1 Witnesses that axioms hold Associator: (hˆg)ˆf ≅ hˆ(gˆf)
2 Coherences between witnesses Pentagonator for 4-fold composition
3 Coherences between coherences Higher associahedron data
n Coherences between level-(n−1) …and so on, forever

IC1–IC13 give you levels 0 and 1 directly. The conjecture claims levels 2, 3, 4, … are generated automatically by the weak reading.

Why the Tower Might Be Automatic

The key idea: the axioms apply at every dimension.

IC9 says composition is associative. Under the weak reading, this gives an associator (a 2-cell). But the associator is itself a cell, and IC9 applies to it too, potentially generating the pentagonator (a 3-cell).

Level 1: IC9 applied to f, g, h gives associator α
Level 2: IC9 applied to associators gives pentagonator π
Level 3: IC9 applied to pentagonators gives the next coherence
Level n: Each level arises from applying the axioms to the previous level's witnesses

If this bootstrapping works, then 13 finitely-stated axioms generate the full infinite tower — a striking case of finite presentation of infinite coherence.

Evidence For the Conjecture

  • Strong adequacy works. The same 13 axioms, read strictly, characterize strict ω-categories exactly (Theorem 6.1). The axioms are the "right" axioms — they capture all the structural laws.
  • The homotopy model validates soundness. A natural class of (∞,1)-categories (boundary-preserving homotopies on topological spaces) satisfies the axioms, with witnesses provided by reparameterizations (Sections 7–8).
  • Normalization succeeds for the free model. The decidability result (Theorem 4.6) shows the free model has clean normal forms. Missing coherences would make the free model "too free," distinguishing terms that should be identified.
  • Precedent: Mac Lane coherence. For monoidal categories, finitely many conditions (pentagon + triangle) generate all coherences. IC1–IC13 could play an analogous role.

Reasons for Caution

  • Finite axioms, infinite tower. It is not obvious that 13 axioms generate all higher coherences. The pentagonator, and every level above it, must arise without being explicitly axiomatized.
  • No bridge to standard models yet. (∞,1)-categories are usually defined via horn-filling (quasicategories) or limit conditions (Segal spaces) — very different from equational axioms. No translation between EqCat∞ and these frameworks exists.
  • Simpson's conjecture. If true (every weak ∞-category is semi-strict), it suggests the coherence landscape is uneven — some axioms need weakening more than others, complicating a uniform weak reading.
  • No algebraic comparison. Batanin–Leinster algebraic weak ∞-categories use globular operads. Relating EqCat∞ to these is an open problem in itself.

Possible Proof Strategy

A proof of the completeness direction might proceed as follows:

Step 1. Given a weak model M of EqCat∞ + invertibility, construct from it a simplicial set (or other combinatorial object).
Step 2. Show this simplicial set satisfies the inner horn-filling condition, making it a quasicategory.
Step 3. Show this construction is an equivalence: every quasicategory arises (up to equivalence) from a weak model.

The hard part is Step 2: extracting horn-fillers from the equational witnesses. The reparameterization mechanism (Section 7.4) suggests how this might work in the homotopy model, but a general argument is needed.

Summary: Weak Adequacy Conjecture

What we know
  • IC1–IC13 characterize strict ω-categories (strong reading)
  • (∞,1)-categories satisfy IC1–IC13 weakly (soundness)
  • Equality in the free model is decidable
What we don't know
  • Whether weak models are necessarily (∞,1)-categories (completeness)
  • Whether the finite axioms generate the full coherence tower
  • How to bridge equational and horn-filling formulations

The Weak Adequacy Conjecture proposes that the answer to all three is yes.

A Second Question: The Effective Topos

The weak adequacy conjecture asks whether EqCat∞ characterizes (∞,1)-categories in general.

But there's a related question: can we find models of EqCat∞ in specific mathematical structures beyond topological spaces?

One particularly interesting candidate is the effective topos (Eff), a fundamental construction in realizability theory and constructive mathematics.

What Is the Effective Topos?

The effective topos (Eff) is a topos constructed from:

  • Realizability: Objects are sets equipped with "realizability" structure from partial recursive functions
  • Constructive logic: Internal logic is intuitionistic, not classical
  • Computational content: Proofs carry algorithmic information

Eff is a 1-topos — it has objects and morphisms with good categorical structure (limits, colimits, exponentials). But it doesn't inherently have higher-dimensional structure.

Key property: Eff is neither Set (classical sets) nor Top (topological spaces), yet it's a rich mathematical universe with its own internal logic.

Conjecture 2: Internal ∞-Category in Eff

Conjecture

There exists an internal ∞-category in the effective topos Eff that validates the axioms of EqCat∞ under the weak reading.

More precisely: there is a model of EqCat∞ whose underlying structure lives internally in Eff, with cells at each dimension being objects of Eff, and composition/identity operations being morphisms in Eff.

This would show that equational ∞-categorical reasoning is compatible with realizability and constructive mathematics, not just classical topology and homotopy theory.

Why the Eff Conjecture Matters

  • Bridges realizability and higher categories. Eff embodies computational/constructive thinking. An ∞-category in Eff would mean higher-categorical reasoning has computational content.
  • Tests the generality of EqCat∞. The axioms were designed for topological models. If they work in Eff, they're more universal than initially apparent.
  • Potential for mechanization. Eff's constructive nature aligns with proof assistants. A model in Eff could lead to computational interpretations of ∞-categorical proofs.
  • Connection to HoTT. Homotopy Type Theory seeks to combine higher categories with type theory. Realizability toposes like Eff provide constructive models of type theories, suggesting potential bridges.

Challenges

  • Eff is a 1-topos, not ∞-topos. There's no built-in higher structure. Would need to construct simplicial objects or some other internal model of ∞-categories.
  • The weak reading assumes topological homotopy. The paper's weak semantics uses continuous maps In → X. Eff doesn't have this structure — need a different notion of "witness."
  • No obvious construction. Unlike topological spaces (which naturally have homotopies), there's no clear recipe for building cells and witnesses in Eff.
  • Eff doesn't model HoTT. Eff doesn't satisfy univalence. If HoTT is the "right" internal language for ∞-categories, Eff might not have the necessary structure.

Possible Approaches

Several strategies could be attempted:

Approach 1: Simplicial objects. Construct the ∞-category as a simplicial object internal to Eff. Prove it satisfies Segal conditions and witnesses the EqCat∞ axioms.
Approach 2: Algebraic weak ∞-categories. Use Batanin/Leinster's globular operads framework. Show Eff supports the necessary operad structure.
Approach 3: Realizability interpretation. Define cells as realizability structures. Witnesses are realized by partial recursive functions. Composition is a computable operation.
Approach 4: Bridge via strong reading. The free model (strong reading) is already computational. Perhaps a realizability interpretation exists there, and the weak reading emerges by internalizing the quotient in Eff.

Two Conjectures, One Framework

Conjecture 1: Weak Adequacy

Under the weak reading, EqCat∞ + invertibility ≥ 2 characterizes exactly the (∞,1)-categories.

Status: Soundness largely proved. Completeness open.

Conjecture 2: Eff Model

There exists an internal ∞-category in Eff validating EqCat∞.

Status: Completely open. No construction known.

Both conjectures test the scope and power of EqCat∞ as a foundation for higher-categorical reasoning.
